Foundation

In late 1886, a gaggle of workers from the Woolwich Arsenal Armament Factory decided to form a football team. They called themselves Dial Square as a reference to the sundial atop the entrance to the factory. On December 11, 1886, Dial Square romped to a 6-0 victory over Eastern Wanderers; the first game in their initial guise. Shortly afterwards the name 'Royal Arsenal' was adopted.

The Chapman Era

When Henry Norris decided that Arsenal needed a new manager in the summer of 1925, he could never have anticipated the impact of his next appointment. Herbert Chapman, who had won the FA Cup and two Division One titles with Huddersfield Town in the early Twenties, agreed to join the Gunners in the summer of 1925.

The Srventies

The 1970s started superbly with European glory and the 'Double' and ended pretty well with a string of Cup Finals, however the middle years were deeply disappointing.

Graham's Glory Year's

In May 1986 George Graham was installed as Arsenal manager. Within a year he had delivered the Club's first silverware since the FA Cup in 1979.

The Wenger Era

Following George Graham's departure, Arsenal were entrusted to an unknown Frenchman who would change the Club forever.
